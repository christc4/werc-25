Goal

If you've never gotten your feet wet with ergonomics, then the nitpicks and ramblings of this blog-post will sound totally inconsequential and best reserved for a hobbyist forum

The goal of writing this is to convince you that the 'quirky' and 'novel' peripheral designs of long-ago actually have merit in computer ergonomics and efficiency, and aren't just historical curiosities to become viral in YouTube videos

So as you read through consider the following words of advice

  • Repetitive-most actions, should be allocated to and performed by one key
    • Single key-press as opposed to chord/layer
  • With programmability, the possibilites are infinite
    • We're talking macro's, shortcuts, bindings etc.

What did we lose and why?

Media buttons

As Xah Lee puts it, media buttons were once in 'vogue' then fell out of fashion

The Kensington Expert, used to sport 6 media buttons which could be assigned different functions

Such genes weren't passed down to it's modern-day descendant

Why have they disappeared?

The ''buttons'' were hard to press and mushy like peas…

And… such was the case with many appearances of media buttons throughout out the '90s which saw them fall out of popularity quickly

Any savings made would've no doubt been offset by a chorus of user complaints
